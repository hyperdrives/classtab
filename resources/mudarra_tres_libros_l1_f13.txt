"Fantasía que contrahaze la harpa en la manera de Ludovico"

Music by Alonso Mudarra (ca.1510+1580)
in "Tres Libros de Música en cifras para vihuela" Sevilla, 1546

Intabulation and fingering by MH Arnoult <m_arnoult@hotmail.com> May 2002
Sources: Guitar transcription (unknown)
         Vihuela tablature (A. Alexander ed.)

Note: The title of this Fantasia, "imitating the harp in the style of
      Ludovico" refers to the improvisation skills of the harpist
      Ludovico, not to his instrument (such a nice one by the way:
      nothing else but open strings...).
      On the original tablature, below stave 61, there is a note reading:
      "Desde aqui fasta acerca del final hay algunas falsas ; tañiéndose
      bien no paracen mal." Which could translate: "From here till near
      the end, there are a few false notes; well played, they don't sound
      too bad."

General Comments:
The first section of the piece (bars 1-53) is quite straightforward once
you master the scale patterns. The second section  is a bit more tricky:
the first part (bars 54-60) is not too difficult however, consisting of
slightly modified G, D and C chords positions. The final part (bars 61-73)
is a bit more complex to handle correctly: I would suggest to play first
the melody on the bass strings alone, and try to keep its rythm pattern in
mind when working on the rest, at a slow pace as a start.

Tuning:
The original piece was written for the vihuela da mano, a Spanish
Renaissance instrument of the lute family: the shape is quite similar
to our modern guitar, with a smaller size however. There were six sets
of double strings, tuned in G: G C F A D g, which corresponds to a
F#drop tuning with a capo on the third fret on a modern guitar:
E A D F# B e; the use of the capo however is optional.

Tempo:
Indicated as "Compas Apresurado" which means "Hurried Scales". So play it
fast, but not too fast on the first crotchets however, otherwise it will be
a bit hard to cope with quavers.

Time signature: 4/4, but bars 26, 33-35 & 40-41 are in 3/2.

Notation Legend:
The note length is indicated above staves, as follows:
h: half note / minim
q: quarter / crotchet
e: 8th / quaver
s: 16th / semi-quaver
.: dotted note
|: beat

Those durations correspond to the higher notes, and not to the bass line
(whose duration is merely indicated by sustain "=="). I hope it will be
useful, but I do know however that it is not completely accurate: that's
just an attempt to make things easier...
Within a measure, I have indicated  the duration of the first note, and
then only the changes.

 Alonso Mudarra - Fantasia

 Tabbed by: The_S (May 2003)

 Based on the tab by: MH Arnoult (He seemed to know what he was doing)

 The_S:   I heard this piece whilst listening to the first classical
          guitar CD I ever got, called Spanish Guitar Music - John Williams
          Unfortunately, I don't have the sheet music for it, so I don't
          know exactly how Mudarra wanted it played.
          What I do know, is that John Williams plays the fast notes toward
          the start using different strings. This makes the song sound
          dreamy, like a harp. So I figured out a way to do the same.
          You will have to stretch a little, but nothing extreme, and the
          last part is very tricky. Follow the bass notes first, then
          coordinate the rest. Good luck.

 Tuning: EADGBE (standard)

 4/4
