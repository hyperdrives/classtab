p = pulgar = thumb
i = indice = index
m = medio = middle
a = annular = ring

Scarborough Fair - Suspended Bass Note
"Scarborough fair" is an example of how the classical guitar can sound like
two instruments. With the suspended bass and running lead, the guitar produces
two voices (Bass and treble).

Technique: This piece is a slow piece. The challenge is to hold bass note which
is struck with the thumb - mostly A (open 5th string). Try and suspend the bass
as long as possible. Two notes are plucked with the thumb and middle (pm) finger
most of the time.

Tip: A good solo guitar piece always contain 2 or more voices. Having voice
diversity broadens the guitar's musical capability.

Legend
------

left hand fingering below tab      right hand fingering above tab
1 - index finger                   p - thumb
2 - middle finger                  i - index finger
3 - ring finger                    m - middle finger
4 - little finger                  a - ring finger

=    - note held
/    - slide up
arp. - arpeggiate - ripple upwards (string order 6,3,2,1)
F.   - (above the tab) Fermata - pause, notes held for longer than their value
g    - grace note  - acciaccatura - the note is played immediately before or
       at the same time as the following note, and immediately released
p    - pull off
<12> - natural harmonic, open string, left hand points to the 12th fret
CII_ - full barre on the 2nd fret with the 1st finger
