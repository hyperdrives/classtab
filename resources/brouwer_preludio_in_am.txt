Notes

measure 21: the sheet shows a barre on the 2nd fret at the start of the measure,
with the G on the 1st string played with the 2nd finger followed by a pull-off;
however it also has F (6th string, 1st fret) in the bass, but with no fingering;
that indicates that it should be F# (6th string, 2nd fret), not F; and the video
clearly shows the barre extending to the 2nd fret (not the first)

Legend

===    held note
p      pull-off
h      hammer-on
/      slide up
\      slide down
<12>   natural harmonic - play open string, touch point on 12th fret

(
)      arpeggiate - strum from the lowest string to the highest string
(
)

< - .  crescendo
. - >  decrescendo
/.\    fermata (long pause)
.      staccato
<      accent
,      breathe (slight pause)
ff     fortissimo (very loud)
f      forte (loud)
p      piano (soft)
pp     pianissimo (very soft)

Allegro ritmico - quickly and rhythmically
cresc. (crescendo) - becoming gradually louder
golpe - flamenco technique where player taps soundboard with their fingers
metal. (metalico) - metallic - play near the bridge (sul ponticello)
molto - much, very
molto sonoro - very sonorously, very resonant, with a very rich tone
pizz. (pizzicato) - (strings are plucked, rather than stroked)
Son nat. - play naturally (negates a previous pizzicato)
sub. (subito) - suddenly
