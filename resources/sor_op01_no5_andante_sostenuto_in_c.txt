Notes
-----
Editions used - Six Divertissemens, Meissonnier 51, Paris (RiBS)
                Six Divertimentos, Hebe's Web A10201 (Tecla), (Jeffery, 2000)

"The indication 'Andante' is probably to be taken as meaning 'with movement',
that is, rather faster than it would normally be interpreted today."
- Brian Jeffery

Suggested tempo: Theme - 54 bpm (1/8th notes)
                 Variation - 84 bpm (1/8th notes)
                 Minore - 54 bpm (1/8th notes)

The tied notes between measures 36-37 (C), 37-38 (Ab), 38-39 (B), and 44-45 (C)
have been been added by Jeffery and are not tied in the Messonier edition.

Measure 33 - in Messonier, the 2nd note (B) is an 1/8th note (not dotted).
Measure 37 - in Messonier, the top note of the last chord is G; it should be Ab.
Measure 47 - in the sheet, the C on the 1st beat is a dotted quarter note.

Legend
------

===  - note held
g    - grace note (extra note) - here they are acciaccaturas, and are played as
       pull-offs just before or simultaneous with the main note
h    - hammer-on
p    - pull-off

cresc.  - crescendo - becoming louder
f       - forte - loud
p       - piano - soft
