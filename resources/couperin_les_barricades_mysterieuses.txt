Les Barricades Mysterieuses (The Mysterious Barricades)
By Francois Couperin (1668-1733)

Tablature:  Mike Schmidt
Email:  Michael_T_Schmidt@hotmail.com
Date: July 5th, 2001

Performance Notes:
The main theme is played in the first 9 measures, followed by 3 "couplets"
or variations of the theme.  Using familiar chord shapes (C, G, Am, etc.)
will help with the fingering on the main theme.  After repeating it once,
the 1st Couplet begins.  The main theme is played in between each couplet,
and again at the end.  Brief pauses before each couplet will emphasize the
variation.  The piece can also be played with a capo on the 2nd fret.  The
faster it is played, the livelier it sounds.  Roman numerals below certain
measures mean a barre on that fret. Although there are a few difficult
fingerings, this is a most rewarding piece to learn and play.
