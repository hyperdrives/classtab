Key
---

Allegro, tempo di Fandango - quite fast, at the pace of a Fandango
Lento - slow
Tempo sostenuto - sustained, played smoothly
Vivo - lively

=      note is held through following beat(s)
10p8   pull-off from the 10th fret to the 8th fret
<12>   open string harmonic on the 12th fret
7<19>  7th fret artificial harmonic on the 19th fret
__3__  triplet - 3 note played in the time of 2
pizz   pizzicato (pinched) notes are plucked rather than stroked
1.___  play first time through
2.___  play second time through

left hand fingering below tab
1  - 1st (fore) finger               CII   - barre on 2nd fret with 1st finger
2  - 2nd (index) finger              CIV   - barre on 4th fret with 1st finger
3  - 3rd (ring) finger               CVII  - barre on 7rd fret with 1st finger
4  - 4th (little) finger (pinkie)    CVIII - barre on 8th fret with 1st finger

Comments
--------

Segovia has recorded this piece more than once - the version which i've mostly
relied on was recorded in 1930 (CD reissue EMI CDH761048) - it is played much
faster than Allegro and sounds to be in the key of Fm (not Em) - where the
recording differs from the sheet music (other than between key signatures), the
recording has taken precedence - (however there are also differences between
the recorded versions, and the score appears to contain some errors)
