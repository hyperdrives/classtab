Air On The G String - (Air from BWV 1068 arr Wilhelmj) - JS Bach (1685-1770)
1st version - by Courtney Babbidge, in D (M4 updated 14th June 2005)
2nd version - by Jonathon Clark, combination of 3rd and 4th versions, in C
3th version - by Roger Margido Proeis, in C
4th version - by Grant Floyd, in C
******************************************************************************

I had been scouring the internet for a guitar arrangement of Bach’s
"Air on the G-string" for months before I finally decided to arrange
the piece myself. I found a few arrangements, but they were either
transposed to a different key, or were in the original key, but left
out too many of the voices. What follows is my best attempt at a
complete arrangement. I tried to preserve the integrity of the
counter-point, but some of the passages contained material that was
simply impossible to play with only four fingers and six strings.
In such cases, I omitted the least important voice, or moved certain
notes to a different octave. I made my best effort to keep the
harmonic structure intact.

Air On A G String by Johann Sebastian Bach
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Transcription by Grant Floyd <floyd11@waikato.ac.nz>

The timing is 4/4. I have used spacing to indicate the timing
so that the initial sequence is very strung out with four
beats to the bar and later as the notes become closer the
grouping of notes indicates which are to be played together.
Some bars have a single or very few  notes. If they're at the
start of the bar then allow them to ring throughout that bar
(ie everything is spaced according to time at playing)
All this stuff is pretty self-evident but timing is very
important in this piece, listen to it and you'll see what I
mean.
