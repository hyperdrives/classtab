Notes & Legend
--------------

The lute tuning was probably in G, equivalent to using a capo on the 3rd fret.
The guitar sheet fingering tries to reflect the original lute music, which can
result in almost impossibly large stretches (eg in measure 6).
Sonata 10 is intended to follow on from Sonata 9.
Where an ornament is indicated above an interval or a chord, the ornament is on
the upper note, except in measure 36 where it is on the middle note (D).
There are extra Ds (open 6th string) at the start of the Divino Kaps video,
(which would benefit from a devocalizer). 
The tempo of the Divino Kaps video is 84 bpm (quarter notes);
The tempo of the MIDI is 76 bpm (quarter notes).
