Johan Sebastian Bach - BWV 1006a - Prelude

Standard tuning - E A D G B E
Time - 3/4

Transcription and fingering by Tolur Niotum (niotum@yahoo.com)

I searched for the most effortless still effective fingering mixing information
from several videos on YouTube and tried to get the best out of each. Most of
the job is based on John Williams performance. To people (like me) frightened
by crappy tabs or annoyed by impossible fingerings excerpted from an official
music sheet: this version is REALLY more approachable! I'm a self-taught
guitarist but without practising that much I could manage to play this fairly
good. Just focus on a regular pace in order to keep this river of notes clean.
There are NO pauses, just at the beginning and at the end, so keep fluent
across the piece and improve your legato ability first. IMHO the interpretation
resides in giving a personal tone to phrases instead of alternating pace, which
I'd find pretty ugly for such a piece. That's the reason why to get *brilliant*
I suggest a metronome.
