***********************************************************
Five Piano Pieces D459/459a, III-Adagio, by Frank Schubert
***********************************************************

Transposed to E major and arranged from the piano sheet music
by Maxime Bergeron (mazout360@hotmail.com).

Notes:
- Another of my favorites from Schubert. This took quite a while to
arrange in a way that is playable on guitar without sacrificing too
much of its musicality.
- * The 10--15--19NH section are all played as appogiaturas. The B (12)
on the B string is plucked with the thumb then m i for (10--15) and then the
harmonic, to mimick the original arpeggio.
- ** Similar to the last note, but this time the actual arpeggio can be
played in part along with a grace note.

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U69jKpZ3S6k - Unknown (Piano)
* This is to my knowledge the only arrangement of this piece for the
classical guitar, so no guitar videos yet.

****************************************************************************
measure 81 corrected 28 April 2014: the F# played on the first string should
be played on the second string to allow a (quick) barré over the fourth fret.
****************************************************************************
