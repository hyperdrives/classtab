***************************** Performance Notes ******************************

   /f\ = fermata (hold note for undetermined time)

   |---6s----| triplet sixteenths for one quarter note

   |-3s-| triplet sixteeths for one eighth

   f = forte (loud)
   ff = fortissimo (very loud)
   sfz = sforzando (suddenly loud)
   sffz = suddenly very loud
   mf = mezzoforte = (medium loud)
   p = piano (soft)
   lent = slow
   piu mosso = a little more
               (usually interpreted as much faster than previous tempo)
   Poco meno = a little less
   bien rythme = proper rhythm
                 (the tempo here should be consistent with campanella section)
   pp pianissimo (very soft)
   ppp = very quiet
   dim. e rall. = diminuendo e ralletando 
                  (progressively diminished and slower)
   > = accented (bring that note out)
   cres. = crescendo (progressively louder)
   decrescendo = progressively louder
   __ = tenudo (add weight and space to the note, not volume)

Etude no 11 by Villa Lobos (1929)

key: E minor (Lento - Piu mosso - Animato - Lento)

"This is a favourite recital piece, features a central flurry
of arpeggios, which incorporates the evocative melody presented
in the two outer sections." - Norbert Kraft

**********************
*  HOW  TO  PLAY  ?  *
**********************

Like the Study 8, here, too, the first phrase appears on the bass,
imitating the cello. There is a big contrast between the Brazilian
singing and the slightly impressionist ornamentation that imitates
a harp. The middle section shows a 'campanella' effect using,
sometimes, up to five 'Es' on five different strings.
