Performance Notes
-----------------
* It is easier to play this chord with a 5/6 barré on fret 2 then release the
third string whilst keeping the fingertip on the 5th string second fret to play
the third string as open string right after. You'll get the hang of it ;)

** Try to keep the lower notes but they're hardly hearable on the video so
if it's too difficult just forget that.

*** This may be easier to play with a barré on string 3 to 6, yet this is a
strange and unnatural finger position.

**** This should actually be a low C to end this piece (which is in C major).
Although it's not theoretically wrong to play a E instead to complete the
last C major chord, a more musically rigorous way to end this piece would
be to play the 6th string open string and drop it to C manually...

***** I confess, I cheat a little when I play this chord. After the 4g2 I use
a 4/6 barré on fret 2 and I just skip the third string open string note in
the following arpeggio if I can't release the barré quickly enough.
