Notes and Legend
----------------
The English comedian Eric Morecambe, joking about his piano playing abilities,
said that he played all the right notes, but not necessarily in the right order.
I have tried to put all the right notes in the right order, but they are not
necessarily on the right strings. 

Suggestions for improving this tab are welcome. The sheet doesn't show any left
hand fingering. Where my fingering is unreasonable, please try and find better
alternatives or watch the videos by Otto Tolomen and Gianluigi Bocelli. If that
doesn't help, then perhaps the bottom E in some of the chords could be moved
an octave lower to the open 6th string, whilst other chords can be arpeggiated.
Non-standard tunings are another option, and harmonics may be possible as
in the Otto Tolomen video, which starts something like this -

     1  |   .   |   .   |   .   |   .     |   .   |   .   |   .   |   .
     E|-------------------------0-------|-----------------0---------------|
     B|---------------------------------|----------------------------<12>-|
     G|-9-------9=======----------------|---------9----------<12>---------|
     D|-9-------9=======--------7-------|---------9-------7========-------|
     A|-10------10======--------8-------|---------10------8========-------|
     E|-12------12======--------12------|---------12-----<12>======-------|

There is no key signature, but the piece sounds close to E minor - maybe it is
written in the mode of C Phrygian?
The tempo of the Otto Tolonen video and the MIDI is 54 bpm (quarter notes).
(Apologies for the MIDI. Even had I the time to devote to it, I'm not sure
my skills are up to creating a MIDI which would do the piece justice.)
