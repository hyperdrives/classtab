TAB Explanation:
----------------

This TAB System consists of 12 lines. (most of the time)

Example:

1.  line:   C9--------------------|
2.  line:   p a i m
3.  line:   |       |       |        |       |       |
4.  line: E|------------------------|------------------------|
5.  line: B|--0-------0-------0-----|--0-------0-------0-----|
6.  line: G|------9-------9-------9-|------10------10------10|
7.  line: D|----10------10------10--|----10------10------10--|
8.  line: A|12------12------12------|12------12------12------|
9.  line: E|------------------------|------------------------|
10. line:   4   2 1                  4   2 1
11. line:   cresc------------------| decresc.---------------|
12. line:   P                        F
(and 13.)

- The first line is used to tell us the playing position.
  A ("C12-----------|") means play in fret position 12
  until the line ends with ("|")

- The second line is intended for right hand fingering,
  so when you see ("p a i m"), the notes below are
  played with those fingers.

- The third line gives us the bar number and the beats per bar.
  (" |       |       |       ")
  ("|------------------------|")
  ...means there are 3 beats in that bar. (3/4 time)

- The next six lines represent the six guitar strings,
  beginning with the high e and ending with the low E.
  A number means play the note on that fret.

- The 10th line (with chords sometimes up to 5 additional
  lines, beginning from the top) is for left hand fingering,
  so ("4") means: play the note above with the 4th finger.

- The 11th, 12th (and sometimes 13th) lines are for
  explanations, expressions, techniques,...

- A ("5h6=") means play 5th fret and hammer on to the 6th.
- A ("6p5=") means play 6th fret and pull off to the 5th.

Time signature and note lenghts:
--------------------------------

- The time signature for this piece is 3/4.

- One single line ("-") represents 1/32th so two lines ("--")
  is 1/16th and four lines ("----") is 1/8th and so on...
  After a numer, you'll find this for example:
  ("|5=----------------------|"). It means the note is a
  16th note because it takes two fields.
  ("|5===--------------------|") means the note is a 1/8.
  Things like ("|12----") are still 1/16 unless noted
  somewhere below the tab.
