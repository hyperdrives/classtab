Notes:

The ^ marks indicate the beats in 4/4 time. They line up with fixed-space
fonts. There are some 1/16 notes in here, eg at the end of measure 4.

The chord notation is fairly standard; Do = D diminished, Bb = B-flat.

The run at the end of measure 4: end it with the 2nd finger on the Ab
(the 7th of the Bb7 chord). This allows for the melody on the 2nd string.

Do not barre the last chord on the 7th fret or you may hear an F# which
would screw up the Dm.  It's really a shame to do the whole thing right
and then hear a major chord at the end. (I've done it).

This piece should be fun because you don't hear it on the guitar often,
and it could be done within a couple of weeks or less.

Fairy by Peter L. Tchaikovsky.  I'm sure everybody knows it from "Home Alone".
It's in the key of E minor and is in 2/4 time.

"s" means the note(s) below it are sixteenth notes.  It's a little hard to
accurately transcribe it because of the mix of 8th and 16th notes.  Another
thing to note is that an 8th note is followed by two spaces while a 16th note
is followed by only 1 space. This might help. It's an eerie awesome song...
enjoy!
