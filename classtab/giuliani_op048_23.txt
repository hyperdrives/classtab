Opus 48 is really a source of brilliant ideas, as showed by this study n.23
in which Giuliani turns his attention to several technical aspects, succeeding
at the same time in writing a very beautiful piece. This isn't easy to play;
I advise you to start playing very slowly, one measure at a time, and to
increase the velocity when you master both positions and fingering. I've
included the composer's indications on the tab (mf, sf etc), so don't forget
to respect them:)!

MAURO GIULIANI (BISCEGLIE 1781 - NAPLES 1829)

Time: 3/4
Signature: B minor

Tuning: standard

Allegro con moto

If possible, it's better to avoid the bar in fast passages.
