This is the best version of the anonmous classic "Romanza" I have ever heard.
The inclusion of the F# is simply captivating and adds a darker quality to the
piece. Also the very, very cool measures #39-40 have a challenging stretch but
it produces a beautiful effect. I remembered seeing Christopher Parkening
perform this on the Johnny Carson show. I imagine this might be in one of
Parkening's books. If someone has them, let me know how accurate I was...

Don't forget to repeat the first section.

Paul R. Estrada - Nov 2002
estrada@cosmic.arc.nasa.gov

Legend
------
left hand fingering below the tab
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

order of play - measures 1-59, 1-8, 60-63

=     - note held
arp.  - arpeggiate - ripple upwards (from the lowest note to the highest note)
g     - grace note (slide) - acciaccatura - note is played immediately before
        or at the same time as the following note and is immediately released
p     - pull off
+     - go to the Coda
CIV__ - full barre on the 4th fret with the 1st finger
CVII_ - full barre on the 7th fret with the 1st finger
cIX__ - part barre on the 9th fret with the 1st finger
