A FELICIDADE
as recorded by roland dyens
from the 1999 album "nuages"

music by antonio carlos jobim
arranged by roland dyens
words by vinicius de moraes
transcribed by wim

a very stylised arrangement of the famous bossa tune.  this arrangement is
packed full of semitones.  dyens plays most of those semitones right down near
the bridge so as to get that twangy metal sound.  if you like this effect you
should also check out the south american guitarist jorge morel, he loves the
quirky semitones too, in particular see "bossa in re".

some performance notes...i play all those bass notes in the intro with the
thumb and palm mute, using sweep picking for those fast ones at the end of each
bar (i'm not sure how dyens plays it).  all the staccato chords (indicated with
a dot above the tab) are made by lifting the left hand slightly.  rhythm is
complex to tab but listen to the .mid - if you're familiar with bossa it's a
fairly typical rhythm throughout and the bassline is almost always plodding
quarter notes.  about the tempo: i checked the midi against dyen's recording
and he actually plays ~117 bpm (!) on the nuages album.  however, the score
calls for 100.  in the midi i have used 104 (simply to be as close to 100 as
possible before the trills went all spastic for some wacky technical reason
unbeknownst to me)

hope you enjoy this one  :)
