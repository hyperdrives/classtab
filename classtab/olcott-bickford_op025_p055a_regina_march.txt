Notes and Legend
----------------
The tempo of the Norbert Neunzling vid and the MIDI is 100 bpm (quarter notes).
In the video, the chords are played in the normal manner, not using brackets
as shown in the tab and explained below.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
{from page 25} "Another style of playing chords which is very brilliant and
effective in many places is called the Bracket, also the Half-Bracket. The
Bracket Chord is played by the first finger and thumb {of the right hand}
alone; the first finger curling up with a sharp quick flick toward the center
of the hand at the same moment the thumb strikes the bass note. The indication
for this manner of playing a chord is an {opening square} bracket preceding the
chord ([). In the Half-Bracket, the bass is separated from the main part of the
chord, so that there is no need for the thumb to act with the finger. In this
case the thumb plays its single bass note as usual and the finger plays alone
the notes composing the chord, in the same manner as for the Bracket chord.
When there are more than one of the chords in succession to be played with a
half-bracket, without a bass note between, the first finger should be
alternated with the second finger for the purpose of obtaining both grace and
speed... The indication for the Half-Bracket is {an upside down L}. The {down}
arrows are often used in modern music to indicate this as v..v..v.v.. {etc.}"
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

left hand fingering (below the tab)
1 - index
2 - middle
3 - ring
4 - little

=      - duration, length of note
[      - (above the tab) bracket (see explanation above)
v      - (above the tab) half-bracket (see explanation above)
