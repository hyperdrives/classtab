Notes and Legend
----------------
The tempo of the MIDI and the Smaro Gregoriadou video is 92 bpm (quarter notes).

measure 45 - the sheet has the A on the 1st beat as a 1/4 note (not an 1/8 note)

left hand fingering (below the tab)
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

===   - duration, length of note
-     - (above/below tab) tenuto - note slightly stressed, or held a bit longer
.     - (above the tab) staccato - note cut short, detached
>     - (above the tab) accented, stressed
|-3-| - triplet - 3 notes in the time of 2 notes
^     - strum from the lowest note up to the highest note
V     - strum from the highest note down to the lowest note
F.    - Fermata - pause
h     - hammer-on
p     - (between the notes) pull-off
< <   - (below the tab) crescendo - growing louder
> >   - (below the tab) decrescendo - growing softer
f     - (below the tab) forte - loud
ff    - fortissimo - very loud
mf    - mezzoforte - moderately loud
mp    - mezzopiano - moderately soft
p     - (below the tab) piano - soft
pp    - pianissimo (very quiet)
ppp   - pianississimo - extremely soft
sf    - sforzando - suddenly heavily stressed
CI    - barre on the 1st fret with the 1st finger
cII   - part barre on the 3rd fret with the 1st finger
CII   - barre on the 2nd fret with the 1st finger
cIII  - part barre on the 3rd fret with the 1st finger
cVIII - part barre on the 8th fret with the 1st finger

cantando - in a singing style, graceful
cresc. - crecendo - growing louder
express. - expressively, with expression (?)
meno f - meno forte - less loud, not so loud
poco piu lento - a little more slowly
stentando - delaying, holding back, to play in a laborious manner
Tempo primo - return to the original tempo (speed)
Tempo rubato - the tempo is not fixed but may be varied by the player
