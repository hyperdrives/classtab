Chansons Françaises: Syracuse
Music by Henri Salvador
Arranged for guitar by Roland Dyens
Tablature by Russell Cook (astropig7@gmail.com)

Notes:
* In measures 1–4, barre your pinky across the two highest strings. Also, play
  bass notes with the flesh of your thumb, as opposed to the nail.

* Aside from some odd stretches, this is not a difficult song to play, and it
  sounds quite beautiful for so little effort. Concentrate on holding notes to
  the indicated times, and pay attention to the dynamics.

Key: Db major
Tuning: Db Ab D G B E

===    held note
p      pull-off
h      hammer-on
/      slide up
\      slide down
*      portamento
(      arpeggiate - strum from the lowest string to the highest string
)

\/     arpeggiate from the highest string to the lowest string
AH     artificial harmonic
NH     natural harmonic
g      grace note(s)
pizz.  pizzicato
nat.   naturally (negates a previous pizz.)
< - .  crescendo
. - >  decrescendo
/.\    fermata
-      tenuto
.      staccato
+      mezzo staccato
<      accent
^      heavy accent
~      vibrato
$      Bartok pizzicato
x      tap the note
,      breathe
ff     fortissimo (very loud)
f      forte (loud)
mf     mezzo forte (medium loud)
mp     mezzo piano (medium soft)
p      piano (soft)
pp     pianissimo (very soft)
sub.   subito (suddenly)

pouce pulpé    play with the flesh of your thumb
en dehors      optional
en filigrane   delicately
soave          sweet
soavissimo     very sweet
aérien         airy
