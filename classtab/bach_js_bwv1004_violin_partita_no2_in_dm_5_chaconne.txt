BWV 1004, Violin Partita No 2 in Dm - 5. Ciaccona - Johann Sebastian Bach
********************************************************************************
1st version - by Roger Margido Proeis, updated by Mark Michaelson (2009)
2nd version - by Roger Margido Proeis, updated by Curzio Vasapollo (2002)
3rd version - by Roger Margido Proeis, updated by Dan Shurley (2001)

I re-arranged Bach's Chaconne because I was not satisfied with current available
tablature arrangements.  I went measure by measure and changed accordingly to
the way I felt most comfortable playing and taking into consideration quality of
sound.  I felt the current available arrangements made too much use of open
strings.  I felt you get a better tone by using less open strings and more
versatility when playing certain notes fretted as opposed to open lets you
create more colors and are able to manipulate the sound and how you play those
notes with more of a variety.
You get a crisper tone when play notes fretted instead of open.  I was looking
for that crisper tone when playing Chaconne.  You can sustain the notes
differently when playing the notes fretted as opposed to open.  I see a lot of
tablature transcriptions are making heavy use of open strings which I don't
always agree.  It really depends on the piece of music and how you are trying
to make it sound.
The Chaccone is a very special piece and I felt it deserved special attention.
It's difficult enough to play as it is.  Customizing the tablature transcription
helped me to learn the piece better and kept my motivation and momentum going to
complete the transcription and learning process.
It was a long painful process.  But very worthwhile and I hope that anyone who
tries to play my arrangement can benefit from my time and effort into making the
piece somewhat easier and more sensible to play.  I hope this helps and I
welcome any questions, comments and corrections if need be.  Always a pleasure.
Mark Michaelson
