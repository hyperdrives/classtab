The Merry Widow (La Viuda Alegre, Die lustige Witwe) - Franz Lehár (1870–1948)

The Merry Widow ('Die lustige Witwe' in German) is an operetta by the Austro–
Hungarian composer Franz Lehár. The librettists, Viktor Léon and Leo Stein,
based the story - concerning a rich widow, and her countrymen's attempt to keep
her money in the principality by finding her the right husband - on an 1861
comedy play, L'attaché d'ambassade (The Embassy Attaché) by Henri Meilhac.
The operetta was first performed at the Theater an der Wien in Vienna on the
30 December 1905. It was Lehár's first major success, becoming internationally
the best-known operetta of its era.
(c) Wikipedia

I came across this wonderful transcription for the guitar of the famous
composition by F.Lehár made by Heike Matthiesen a couple of years ago. Since I
was unable to find any music sheets or tabs on the Internet, I tried to tab it
myself directly from the video recording. I am sure you will do justice to this
beautiful piece of music despite the fact that tabs may contain mistakes.
I would be grateful for any additions and corrections.

It is better if you listen to the song by yourself (either the video by Heike
Matthiesen or the original composition) and thus learn the tempo and timing.
