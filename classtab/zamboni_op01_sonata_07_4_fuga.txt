Notes & Legend
--------------

The lute tuning was probably in G, equivalent to using a capo on the 3rd fret.
Zamboni's pieces are sometimes played with the 3 bottom strings tuned an octave
lower. The guitar sheet fingering tries to reflect the original lute music.

The tempo of the Divino Kaps video is 80 bpm (quarter notes).
The tempo of the MIDI is 72 bpm (quarter notes).

measure 10 - in the original manuscript the 12th note is F# -

measure 38 - the E# of the 1st chord is a quarter note in the sheet

===   - note held
arp.  - arpeggiate - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
h     - hammer-on
p     - pull-off
8     - (below the tab) additonal bass note one octave lower in the lute sheet
##    - an ornament is indicated in the original
CII__ - barre on the 2nd fret with the first finger
CIII_ - barre on the 3rd fret with the first finger
CIV__ - barre on the 4th fret with the first finger
CV___ - barre on the 5th fret with the first finger
CVI__ - barre on the 6th fret with the first finger
CVII_ - barre on the 7th fret with the first finger
