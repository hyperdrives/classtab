*************** Performance Notes ***************
*************************************************

The scales are commonly played using rest stroke for a more powerful, deeper
tone and always without slurs until possibly m12.

Measure 12: |-7----(9)7-6-7-(12)10-9-10--13------|- The notes in parenthesis
are grace notes in the score.

For the arpeggio section beginning with the direction "moins", I have included
some left hand fingerings. The movement is primarily parallel, that is, the
hand keeps the same shape up and down the neck. The trickiest part would be m19

               |  e  +  a  |  e  +  a  |  e  +  a  |  e  +  a
            E|-10----------------------8-----------6-----------|-
            B|-------7--------7--------------7--------7--------|-
measure 19: G|----------0--------0--------------0--------0-----|-
            D|----8--------8--------8-----8--------8--------8--|-
            A|-0-----------------------------------------------|-
            E|-------------------------------------------------|-
               4  2  1     2  1     2  4  3  2     1  2     3
                                                   3
Use 4 as a guide finger. Just before the shift, lift 2 and 3, then once 4
shifts plant 3 and 2 in succession as each is prepared to play. It may take
some practice, but the execution produces a smooth melodic line. Also for
the melody in this section, play using rest stroke with the "a" finger where
ever possible. This helps bring the melody out over the arpeggio accompaniment.
The melody switches to the bass starting in mm 22. Use a lot of even vibrato.
Vibrato is often executed in triplets; in a situation like this you end with a
3 against 4 pattern, a rather complicated sycopation for most guitarists, but
it shouldn't take up too much practice time to figure it out. The dynamic
shading is a more complicated subject to discuss. Just listen for tension and
resolution, and changes in mood. Manuel Barrueco's recording of this is a
perfect place to start.


               |  e  +  a  |  e  +  a  |  e  +  a  |----3e-----|
measure 14: E|-12----------------------10=========(10)--13---12|-

==(10) is a tied note, |----3e-----| inidicates triplet eighth notes

At measure 24, the block chords are played staccato, except for the third
chord in that measure, and move mostly in parallel, as well.

       E|-14----------------------
       B|-0--0--------0--------0-- the index finger barres the 10th frets
m29    G|-0-----0--------0-------- on strings E, A, and D. Ring finger
       D|-12-------12-------12---- frets D-12, and Pinky frets E-14
       A|-10----------------------
       E|-10----------------------

Beginning at measure 46, the notes are all hammer-ons and pull-offs and
should be played as fast and intensely as possible. The number of H.O.'s
and P.O.'s indicated are approximate.

All the recordings I've heard, the repeat isn't taken and instead goes on
to 2nd ending, however the 1953 Max Eschig edition, from which this is 
taken, includes the repeat.

a tempo = back to tempo

h = hammer-on

CII = barre chord 2nd fret

2/3CII = barre chord covering 2/3's of the strings

allarg. = allargando = become broader, wider, deeper.

/f\ = fermata

f = forte

rit. = ritardando = a slowing down

sfz = sforzando = suddenly loud

sffz = suddenly very loud

VIF = lively.
The sheet music calls for the scale at this point to be played staccato
