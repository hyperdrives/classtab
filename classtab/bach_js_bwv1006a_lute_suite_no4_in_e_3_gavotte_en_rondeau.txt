           Note: There is at least one other version of this piece on the web
                 that's more accurate with regard to tonality.  This is mainly
                 because I've tabbed this piece primarily in the 1st position.
                 This version is much easier for the beginner; and
                 that's what this site is about.  The purpose here is to offer
                 something not too difficult for the beginner yet a little more
                 interesting than a basic study or minuet. I think it could be
                 included in anyone's repertoire.

From: kevins@ornews.intel.com (Kevin Smith) [email address not active May 2012]

This is Bach's Gavotte En Rondeau from BMV 1006a.  BMV 1006a is a lute
suite adaption of Bach's Violin Partita III (BMV 1006).  "Gavotte" means
it is in the form of a French gavotte dance.  It is usually in 4/4, 4/2,
or 2/2 with a nice even rhythm.  It usually has an elegant but animated
feel.  "Rondeau" means that a main theme is alternated with contrasting
passages.  In this piece, the main theme is stated in measures 0 through
8 and is restated at measures 16, 40, 64, and 92.
